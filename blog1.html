Blog #1: What is Blockchain?

29th sept 2021

Blockchain is a new technology wave that has already started revolutionizing and disrupting business, social and political interactions. It started in 2008 when an anonymous identity called Satoshi Nakamoto introduced Bitcoin as a “Peer to Peer Electronic Cash system”.

Traditional banking or other centralized systems have a central authority and all the users of that application trust that central authority for all the transactions and storage of data. In contrast, Blockchain applications eliminate the need for these third party central authorities. Trust is provided by the technology through the use of decentralization, distribution and consensus based algorithms.

Definition:

A data structure of blocks that are connected together to create a collection of records known as a “Digital Ledger”. Blockchain is a Decentralized and Distributed database that holds transactions in the form of blocks.

Representation of blockchain:

A blockchain is a collection of blocks chained to each other using cryptographic methods (Hashing) as shown below. The blocks contain transactions that happen in the network.

Features of Blockchain:

Blockchain is a disruptive technology and changing the way businesses operate around the world due to the below features

  • Tamper-resistance
  • Decentralized network
  • Security
  • Distributed ledger
  • Consensus based
  • Faster settlement
  • Globally accepted

Industries which can leverage blockchain technology:

Blockchain is a secure way of storing data and because of all the above features of blockchain, many industries are adopting blockchain to increase the efficiency of their operations and to eliminate third party intermediaries. Some of the few industries where blockchain is being used are:

  • Banking
  • Real Estate
  • Voting
  • Healthcare
  • Supply Chain
